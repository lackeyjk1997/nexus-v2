[Sundial AI — Discovery Call — 26 min]
[Participants: Sarah Chen (AE, Nexus/Anthropic), Chris Donovan (Co-founder & CTO, Sundial AI)]

SARAH CHEN: Chris, thanks for reaching out. I know founder time is the scarcest thing there is, so I'll be efficient. Tell me what Sundial does and where Claude fits in your head.

CHRIS DONOVAN: Yeah, so — we're small, like a hundred and forty people, growing fast. We build an AI product for, um, it's a consumer-facing creative assistant, I won't bore you with the pitch. The model is the product. It's not a feature bolted on the side, the model basically is the thing the customer experiences. So picking the right one is kind of an existential decision for us, not a procurement decision.

SARAH CHEN: That's a really important distinction and it changes how I'd want to help you. When the model is the product, what are you optimizing for — quality, cost, latency, control?

CHRIS DONOVAN: Honestly all four and they fight each other. Quality is non-negotiable because it's what users feel. But we're a startup, so cost per interaction matters a lot, and latency — our whole UX is real-time, so a slow response is a dead feature. We've been hacking on whatever credits we had from our accelerator, and it's fine for a prototype, but I can't build a company on "fine for a prototype."

SARAH CHEN: That's fair. Can I ask what's pushing you to make a real decision now versus keep hacking?

CHRIS DONOVAN: We just raised, and the board wants a defensible technical foundation, not a hack. And — the thing that actually worries me, philosophically — the models are improving so fast that I'm scared of building deeply on the wrong abstraction and getting stranded. Like, the pace of releases is dizzying. Every quarter the ground shifts. So part of what I'm buying is a bet on who I want to be tied to for the next two years, not just whose model is best this week.

SARAH CHEN: That's one of the smartest framings of this I've heard from an early-stage founder, honestly — you're picking a trajectory, not a snapshot. That's exactly the right way to think about it. Let me try to be useful on the specifics. What can I get you that would actually de-risk the decision?

CHRIS DONOVAN: Okay, two concrete things. One — and this matters to me a lot — do you have a reference customer that's a startup at our stage? Like a seed-or-A company where the model is the product and the founder shipped on you? I don't want a Fortune 500 case study, those companies have problems I don't have. I want to talk to someone my size who bet on you and didn't regret it.

SARAH CHEN: That's a completely reasonable ask and I want to get it right rather than force-fit you a big logo. I don't have the perfect-match reference at my fingertips right now — let me go find you a founder at genuinely your stage and profile, not just whoever's handy. Give me a couple days on that one.

CHRIS DONOVAN: Appreciate that, the honesty too. Second thing — I need real latency numbers. Not "it's fast." I need the actual p95 latency for the model tier we'd use, at our kind of payload, because if it's slow our product is dead and no quality score saves it. Do you have that?

SARAH CHEN: I want to give you the real p95 for the specific tier and payload shape you'd run, and I'd rather pull the accurate number than quote you something approximate that turns out wrong in your UX. So let me get you that in writing too — the real latency profile, not a brochure number.

CHRIS DONOVAN: That's all I ask. The number, honestly. I've been told "it's fast" by everyone and it means nothing.

SARAH CHEN: You'll get the actual number. Let me ask about scale so I size it right — what's your rough request volume now and where do you think it goes if the product works?

CHRIS DONOVAN: Right now it's modest, we're maybe — I don't want to overstate it. Low millions of calls a month. But if the consumer growth curve hits, that could 10x in two quarters, and that's the scenario I want to plan for, not today's number. I don't want to pick something that's great at our current size and falls over at 10x.

SARAH CHEN: Then let's plan for the 10x, not today. So here's what I'll do: I'll find you a genuinely stage-matched founder reference, get you the real p95 latency for your tier and payload, and a view on how the pricing and capacity scale as you grow into that 10x. Then we talk again with real inputs. Does that match what you need?

CHRIS DONOVAN: That's exactly it. The reference and the latency number are the two things I actually need to move. Everything else I can figure out.

SARAH CHEN: Then those are my homework. Give me a few days and I'll come back with both. Thanks Chris — this was a good one, and congrats on the raise.

CHRIS DONOVAN: Thanks Sarah. Talk soon.

[Call ends]
